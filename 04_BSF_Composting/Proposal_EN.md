## 🚨 Policy Context: Taiwan's 2027 Full Pig-Feed Ban (African Swine Fever)

> The market opportunity for this project is directly created by a confirmed, major regulatory shift — confirmed by multiple official Taiwanese government sources in 2025–2026.

In **October 2025**, Taiwan experienced an African Swine Fever (ASF) outbreak, traced directly to **unsterilized kitchen waste (廚餘)** fed to pigs on commercial farms. In response, the Executive Yuan enacted a phased national ban:

| Phase | Timeline | Rule |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Phase 1 | Jan 1, 2026 | Household kitchen waste immediately banned as pig feed |
| Phase 2 | All of 2026 | Business food waste permitted under strict conditions only |
| **Full Ban** | **Jan 1, 2027** | **ALL food waste as pig feed completely prohibited nationwide** |

**The Market Vacuum**: Taiwan generates **2,115 tonnes/day** of food waste. Of this, ~**731 tonnes/day** previously went to pig farms and now urgently needs an alternative processing pathway. **Taiwan's Ministry of Environment officially lists Black Soldier Fly cultivation as 1 of only 4 approved alternative pathways.**

## 🏛️ 1. Social Problem Analysis

### ⚠️ The Three-Layer Food Waste Crisis

**Crisis 1: The Pig-Feed Channel is Permanently Closed**
Starting 2027, the largest single urban food waste disposal channel — pig farms — is shut down by law. 731 tonnes/day of business food waste has no legal home. The government is actively seeking operators to process this volume.

**Crisis 2: High-Lipid Waste Has No Good Solution**
- Taiwan's iconic night markets (Shilin, Raohe, Fengjia, Ningxia) generate waste with **high lipid and salt content** (fried foods, broths, soy-heavy dishes) that kills traditional composting bacteria.
- Urban food waste must travel 30–80 km to rural composting plants, creating additional carbon emissions.

**Crisis 3: Protein Import Dependence**
- Taiwan imports significant volumes of fishmeal (魚粉) for its aquaculture sector — one of Asia's largest — with prices subject to global volatility.
- Taiwan's Fisheries Research Institute confirmed BSF larvae as a full fishmeal replacement for Japanese eel aquaculture with **no negative impact on growth or meat quality**.

### 🎯 Target Demographic
- **City Market Associations**: Shilin, Ningxia, Luodong, and major commercial food waste generators.
- **Aquaculture Farmers**: Tilapia and shrimp producers needing local, sustainable, cost-competitive feed.

## 🚀 2. Company Mission & Solution

> **"Turn tomorrow's pig-feed ban into today's protein revolution — in 24 hours."**

### Vision
To become Taiwan's primary commercial food waste processor post-2027, substituting 20% of Taiwan's imported fishmeal with locally-engineered BSF protein by 2030.

### The Solution: Decentralized Bio-Modular Hubs

1. **Black Soldier Fly (BSF) Larvae Reactor**: 24/7 modular aerobic hubs using BSF larvae (*Hermetia illucens*) to reduce waste mass by **70% in under 12 days**, producing high-value protein biomass.
2. **Whole-Insect Protein Feed**: Turning dried larvae into **40% crude protein** pellets for Taiwan's poultry and aquaculture industries — a direct, government-backed fishmeal substitute.
3. **BSF Frass (Organic Fertilizer)**: A slow-release, odorless, pathogen-free fertilizer from larval excrement — ideal for Taiwan's urban farming movement under the "Bio-Gold" brand.

### Why BSF Wins vs. The Other 3 Official Government Alternatives

## 📊 3. Business Model & Innovation

### Decentralized "Bio-Container" Design
Shipping-container-sized BSF reactors placed **within 500 meters** of major night market clusters, neutralizing waste at source. IoT sensor suite monitors temperature (28–32°C), humidity (60–70%), and CO₂ in real-time.

### Multi-Stream Revenue (Per Hub, Annualized)

| Product | Annual Volume | Price/Tonne | Annual Revenue |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| BSFL Protein Meal | 50 tonnes | ~$1,500 USD | $75,000 USD |
| BSF Frass Fertilizer | 150 tonnes | ~$300 USD | $45,000 USD |
| Tipping Fee (from markets) | 700 tonnes | ~$30 USD | $21,000 USD |
| **Total Revenue/Hub** | — | — | **~$141,000 USD** |

> **CAPEX per hub**: ~$120,000–$180,000 USD | **Payback period**: ~18–30 months

### Government Subsidy Leverage
- **Per-pig-head feed transition subsidy**: NT$3,600/head — as pig farms exit waste processing, B2G contracts emerge for BSF operators to take over collections.
- **Ministry of Environment target**: 2,119 tonnes/day processing capacity by end of 2027 — active funding available for new food waste processors.
- **Council of Agriculture (農業部) biotech grants**: Urban BSF facilities may qualify.
