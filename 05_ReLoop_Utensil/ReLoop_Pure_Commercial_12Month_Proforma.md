## 🔬 Critical Financial Metrics (Pure Commercial)

### 1. The "Death Valley" (Months 1-2)
The business will lose roughly **NT$12,700** before reaching Month 3. You need to have this "Runway" cash in the bank to avoid bankruptcy. 

### 2. High-Capacity OpEx Breakdown (Month 12)
*   **Revenue**: 30,000 meals/month @ $11 (Avg) = $330,000.
*   **Variable Costs**:
    *   Labor (6 hrs/day): $35,280.
    *   Loss Reserve (0.5% due to better tech): $18,000.
    *   Consumables: $15,000.
*   **Fixed Costs**:
    *   Rent: $8,000.
    *   Utils: $3,000.
*   **Net Profit Margin**: **51%**.

### 3. Depreciation Buffer
We assume the 250 boxes buy-in lasts for 1 year (Wear & Tear). Every year, we reinvest **NT$30,000** of our profit to "Refresh" the inventory. Even with this cost, the business remains extremely profitable at scale.

## 🚩 Strategic Verdict: "The Scale or Die" Rule
In a Pure Commercial model with high rent, **ReLoop cannot stay small**. Small scale (100 units) is only for **System Validation**. The true business logic only works at **300+ units**, where the fixed rent becomes less than 10% of total costs.

**This is the proof that ReLoop is ready for the Yunus Award: It is a self-funding engine that can pay its own rent and generate millions for social reinvestment within 1 year.**
