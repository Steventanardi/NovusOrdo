## 3. High-Level Hypotheses & Pivots

### Hypothesis A: The "Loss-to-Sale" Pivot
*   **Hypothesis**: If students do not return boxes, the business will collapse.
*   **Worst-Case Result**: Losing 5 boxes/day = -NT$1,684 deficit.
*   **The Pivot**: If Loss > 2%, initiate **Mandatory Deposit (NT$150)**.
    *   *New Logic*: 1 box lost = NT$150 gained. 
    *   *Result*: The "Worst Case" deficit drops to **profit** because you are "selling" boxes at $150 that cost you $120.

### Hypothesis B: The "Vendor Savings" Hook
*   **Hypothesis**: Vendors will resist the $4 service fee.
*   **Proof**: Current disposable costs are $5 - $6. At $4, the vendor **saves 20% to 33%**.
*   **Action**: Use the saved NT$1 to fund a **"Vendor Green Star"** marketing campaign, increasing their sales by 5-10%.

### Hypothesis C: The "Scale Efficiency" Efficiency
*   **Hypothesis**: Manual washing is too slow for campus growth.
*   **Vision**: Once servings > 500, we rent an industrial hood dishwasher.
*   **Result**: Labor per unit drops from **NT$3.92** to **NT$1.17**. Profit margin expands by **235%**.
