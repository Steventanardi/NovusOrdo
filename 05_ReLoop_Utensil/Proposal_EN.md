## 🚨 Policy Context: Taiwan's Single-Use Tableware Bans

Taiwan's Ministry of Environment has progressively banned single-use plastic tableware from restaurants and food establishments:
- **August 2023**: Dine-in restaurants of all sizes are banned from providing single-use plastic bowls, plates, cups, and meal boxes.
- **September 2024**: Nationwide ban on single-use plastic beverage cups.
- **2030 Target**: 5% reduction in single-use petrochemical plastic products (using 2024 baseline).

Yet **10,176 bento (便當) and canteen shops** with annual revenue of **NT$31.52 billion** (2024, fastest-growing food sector at +21.7% YoY) continue to generate billions of single-use containers — even paper bento boxes remain difficult to recycle once oil-contaminated. **This is the gap ReLoop fills.**

## 🏛️ 1. Social Problem Analysis

### ⚠️ The Bento Box Waste Crisis — A Hidden Flood

Taiwan's bento restaurant sector is booming, but with it comes a staggering waste problem:

| Metric | Value | Source |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Bento & canteen shop count (2024) | **10,176 stores** | Ministry of Finance Taiwan |
| Annual bento sector revenue (2024) | **NT$31.52 billion** | Ministry of Finance Taiwan |
| YoY growth rate (fastest in food sector) | **+21.7%** | Manager Today, 2024 |
| Taiwan Rail alone — bento sold/year | **9.31 million units** | Business Today, 2024 |
| Paper bento box recycling rate | Poor — oil contamination renders most **unrecyclable** | MoEnv Taiwan |
| Single-use plastic tableware ban | Active since Aug 2023 — but enforcement gaps remain | MoEnv Taiwan |

**The core problem**: The standard bento box — whether plastic PP tray, paper-coated box, or foam container — is used once, for ~15 minutes of eating, then discarded. Even "paper" bento boxes are laminated with PE film to prevent oil seepage, making them nearly impossible to recycle at scale.

### The Three Failure Modes of Current Solutions
1. **Bring-Your-Own (BYO)**: Culturally inconsistent for bento takeout; consumers don't carry containers to canteens.
2. **Dine-in only reusables**: Doesn't solve the canteen takeout-dominant model where 60%+ meals leave the store.
3. **Centralized composting / incineration**: High carbon transport cost; end-of-pipe solution that doesn't reduce production.

### 🎯 Target Segments
- **Primary**: Bento / 便當 canteen restaurants (10,176 stores nationally).
- **Secondary**: Office district lunch delivery clusters (Xinyi, Zhongshan, Neihu in Taipei; Taichung Science Park; Hsinchu Biomedical Science Park).
- **Tertiary**: University & school cafeteria networks.

## 🚀 2. Company Mission & Solution

> **"Every bento box should go home — and come back."**

### Vision
By 2030, make **ReLoop containers** the default choice for Taiwan's bento economy — eliminating 500 million single-use containers annually and creating a new circular asset class from reusable food service ware.

### The Solution: ReLoop Utensil-as-a-Service (UaaS)

**ReLoop** operates a closed-loop reusable container ecosystem tailored to the high-frequency, takeout-dominant bento canteen model. Unlike BYO or dine-in-only solutions, ReLoop's three-pillar system works within existing consumer behavior:

#### Pillar 1 — The ReLoop Smart Container
- **Material**: Food-grade CPET (Crystallised PET) — microwave-safe, heat-resistant to 220°C, dishwasher-safe, and rated for **500+ wash cycles** before degradation.
- **Design**: Standardized modular sizes fitting all bento portion types (S/M/L + divider tray). RFID tag embedded in lid for tracking.
- **Branding**: Clean, attractive matte design. "ReLoop" mark signifies hygiene-certified circular container.

#### Pillar 2 — The Smart Return Network
- **Return Stations**: Compact, self-cleaning smart return boxes (~60×40×40 cm) placed at:
  - Partner bento stores (returning to where you bought)
  - Convenience stores (7-Eleven, FamilyMart — existing partnerships model like GoodToGo)
  - Office building lobbies (targeting 20+ story office complexes)
  - University cafeteria gates
- **Technology**: QR code scan-to-return via LINE bot (zero app download friction). IoT-enabled fill-level monitoring alerts logistics team.
- **Rule**: Return within **3 days** or NT$150 hold is charged to LINE Pay / credit card on file.

#### Pillar 3 — Centralized Washing & Logistics Hub
- Industrial-grade commercial dishwashers (85°C+ sanitization cycle, SGS-certified).
- "Micro-depot" washing hubs within 10 km of restaurant clusters, minimizing transport carbon.
- Reverse logistics: collection van routes optimized using AI routing (same principle as BSF frass delivery).

## 📊 3. Business Model & Innovation

### "YouBike for Bento Boxes" — The Positioning

ReLoop is positioned as the **YouBike (公共自行車) of food service containers**: a shared urban infrastructure that replaces individual ownership with network access. Just as YouBike made cycling convenient without owning a bike, ReLoop makes sustainable eating convenient without owning a container.

### Three Revenue Streams

| Revenue Stream | Mechanism | Margin Profile |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **B2B Restaurant Subscription** | Monthly fee per store for container supply, washing, and logistics. NT$1,500–3,000/store/month | Recurring — High |
| **B2B Corporate ESG Package** | Enterprise contracts for office lunch delivery programs. Includes ESG carbon report per quarter | Recurring — High |
| **B2G Government Partnership** | Subsidized deployment in public schools, universities, government canteens under MoEnv circular economy programs | Project-based |

### Unit Economics (Per Hub — 50 Partner Stores)

| KPI | Target |
| :--- | :--- |
| Containers in circulation per hub | 5,000 units |
| Average daily transactions | 500 bento meals/day |
| Container return rate target | ≥90% |
| Container wash-cycle lifespan | 500 cycles (~1.4 years at daily use) |
| **Revenue per hub/month** | **~NT$100,000–150,000** |
| **OPEX per hub/month** | **~NT$60,000–80,000** |
| **Net margin per hub** | **~30–40%** |

### LCA Break-Even Analysis

| Container Type | Break-Even (uses) | Carbon Savings (after BE) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Reusable CPET vs. single-use PP tray | **~13 uses** | ~65g CO₂ avoided per meal |
| Reusable CPET vs. paper+PE bento box | **~8 uses** | ~80g CO₂ avoided per meal |
| Stainless steel (premium tier) | ~50 uses | ~110g CO₂ avoided per meal after BE |

> At 500+ cycle lifespan, each ReLoop container avoids the equivalent of **487 single-use containers** from being produced and disposed. At scale of 1M containers: equivalent to planting **~3,800 trees** annually in carbon offset terms.

### Innovation: The ReLoop ESG Dashboard

Every partner restaurant and corporate client receives a **real-time circular economy dashboard** (web + LINE bot) showing:
- Containers borrowed vs. returned this week
- CO₂ avoided (kg)
- Equivalent trees planted
- Certification badge for marketing: "ReLoop Partner — 循環餐具認證"

This ESG data is exportable for CSR reports, directly serving the growing demand from listed companies to show Scope 3 supply chain emissions reductions.

## 📈 5. Financial & Strategic Sustainability

### Container Cost vs. Disposable Comparison

| | Single-Use PP Tray | Paper Bento Box | **ReLoop CPET (per use)** |
| :--- | :---: | :---: | :---: |
| Per-unit container cost | NT$3–5 | NT$2–4 | **NT$0.80–1.20** (at 500 cycles) |
| Washing + logistics cost | — | — | NT$0.50–0.80 |
| **Total cost to restaurant per meal** | NT$3–5 | NT$2–4 | **NT$1.30–2.00 ✅ Cheaper** |

> ReLoop is **cost-competitive from Day 1** for high-volume bento stores using 200+ containers/day. Savings grow with volume.

### Strategic Roadmap

- **Year 1 (Pilot)**: 50 partner stores in Taipei (Zhongshan + Da'an District). 5,000 containers. 1 wash hub. Prove return rate ≥85%.
- **Year 2**: Expand to Taichung and Hsinchu. Launch corporate ESG package. 500 stores, 50,000 containers. 5 wash hubs.
- **Year 3**: B2G contract with Ministry of Education for school cafeteria network (4,000+ schools nationwide).
- **Year 5**: 3,000 partner stores across Greater Taiwan. 300,000 containers in circulation. **500 million single-use containers eliminated annually.**

### Competitive Moat
- **Network effect**: The more return stations, the higher return rates → lower cost per use → more restaurants join → more stations.
- **Data moat**: ReLoop accumulates Taiwan's only real-time bento consumption dataset — valuable for MoEnv policy and restaurant industry analytics.
- **Regulatory tailwind**: Ministry of Environment's single-use tableware restrictions tighten every year, making ReLoop not optional but inevitable.
