# Small-Scale Energy Tile Proposal: School Corridor Pilot

[<img src="https://img.shields.io/badge/Home%20-%20README-000000?style=for-the-badge&logo=home&logoColor=white">](../README.md)

This proposal outlines a **Small-Scale (10-Tile)** pilot installation designed for a high-traffic **school corridor**, **dorm entryway**, or **personal home gym** setting.

## 1. Project Specifications
- **Area**: 2.5 square meters (approx. 27 sq ft).
- **Technology**: Electro-Mechanical Induction.
- **Output**: 5–8 Watts per footstep.
- **Estimated Load**: 1,000 footsteps per day (School corridor average).

## 2. Estimated Budget (Small-Scale)

| **Total Estimated Cost** | | **$430,000 NTD** |

---

## 3. Why This Scale? (Advantages)
- **Personal/School Use**: This scale is perfect for a **Master's Thesis** or a **Sustainability Grant** project.
- **Low Risk**: The lower budget ($13,000 USD approx.) makes it easier to gain approval from school boards or small startup incubators.
- **Public Engagement**: Even 10 tiles can power an interactive display or a "phone charging station," providing high visibility with low infrastructure impact.

---

## 4. Implementation (School/Dorm Setting)
1.  **Placement**: Choose the entrance of the dormitory or the main hallway of the library.
2.  **Display**: Install a small screen showing: *"Total Energy Generated: 120 Wh (Equivalent to charging 5 phones)."*
3.  **Gamification**: Introduce a class competition: *"The class with the most footsteps generates the most green energy!"*
