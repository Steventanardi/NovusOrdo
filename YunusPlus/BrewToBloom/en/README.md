# Brew-to-Bloom: Sorghum Grain Circular Economy
### A Social Business for Kinmen's Ecological Restoration

## 1. Social Issue (Practical Context)
Kinmen produces over 20,000 tons of sorghum distillery grains annually. While the major distillery (KKL) handles most, small-scale producers often lack efficient waste management. Simultaneously, Kinmen's soil is nutrient-depleted due to continuous sorghum farming. Chemical fertilizers are expensive to import and harm local ecology.

## 2. Practical Solution
Establishing a **Micro-Circular Hub** (MCH). We use a modular container-based system to transform fresh distillery grains into high-quality organic bio-fertilizer. To ensure it is "not too good to be true," we focus on a **village-level pilot** starting with only 2-3 local partner distilleries.

## 3. Operational Workflow (Step-by-Step)
1. **Sourcing**: Bi-weekly collection of fresh grains from small distilleries.
2. **Preprocessing**: Mixing grains with local agricultural waste (crushed stalks/leaves) to balance C/N ratio.
3. **Biological Processing**: Using **Black Soldier Fly (BSF)** larvae. *Note: Odor control is managed via biological filters to ensure rural neighbors are not disturbed.*
4. **Harvesting**: Separating the "frass" (fertilizer) from the larvae.
5. **Distribution**: Direct delivery to 10-15 "Beta Farmers" at a 30% discount vs market organic fertilizer.

## 4. Key Implementation Steps (The "How-To")
- **Phase A (Research/Trust)**: Spend 2 months working with the Kinmen Farmers' Association to identify the most nutrient-depleted plots.
- **Phase B (Modular Setup)**: Deploy one 20ft converted container. This minimizes initial overhead and allows for rapid "failure/adjustment."
- **Phase C (Validation)**: Conduct a side-by-side growth test (Bio-fertilizer vs. Control) to provide hard proof to skeptical farmers.

## 5. Risks & Mitigation (Realism Check)
- **Risk**: High humidity in Kinmen might stall BSF growth.
- **Mitigation**: Using sensors and small dehumidifiers powered by solar panels on the container roof.
- **Risk**: Farmers' reluctance to switch fertilizers.
- **Mitigation**: Start with a "Pay-if-it-works" model.

## 6. Business & Social Impact
- **Profitability**: Revenue comes from selling dried larvae (protein feed) and high-end compost.
- **Yunus Alignment**: Profits are reinvested to hire 2 part-time local seniors.
