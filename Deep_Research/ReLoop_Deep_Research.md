## 📌 Executive Summary

Taiwan's bento restaurant sector—10,176 stores generating NT$31.52 billion in 2024 sales (+21.7% YoY)—is the fastest-growing food sector in Taiwan. Yet it operates on a fundamentally linear model: every meal produces one single-use container, used for ~15 minutes, then discarded. Even "paper" bento boxes are PE-film laminated and nearly impossible to recycle once contaminated by food grease.

ReLoop proposes a **Utensil-as-a-Service (UaaS)** model: a closed-loop shared container ecosystem placed at the intersection of Taiwan's regulatory push against single-use plastics and the booming bento canteen sector.

**Key Insight**: Taiwan's bento sector is not slowing down. With inflation driving consumers towards affordable meal solutions and post-COVID dining behavior normalized around takeout, bento stores are accelerating—bringing a proportionally increasing stream of single-use container waste with them.

### 1.2 Container Waste Problem

| Container Type | Issue | Recyclability |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Polypropylene (PP) #5 tray | Theoretically recyclable, but often contaminated | Low — requires rinsing |
| Paper bento box (PE-lined) | Cannot be recycled — PE film bonds to paper layers | **Near zero** in practice |
| Expanded Polystyrene (EPS) foam | Banned in many jurisdictions; lightweight but non-recyclable | **Zero** |
| CPET (Crystallized PET) | Recyclable in industrial stream; rare in Taiwan bento | High if collected properly |

Taiwan's own statistics confirm: even with 85%+ paper tableware recycling rates reported, a significant fraction of contaminated bento containers are incinerated due to food grease contamination that renders them unsortable.

**The math**: If each of Taiwan's 10,176 bento stores sells an average of 200 meals/day × 365 days × 1 container = **~743 million containers/year** generated by the bento sector alone. At current single-use waste rates, this is essentially a one-way flow to incineration.

## 🇹🇼 Part II — Taiwan Regulatory Landscape

### 2.1 Progressive Single-Use Tableware Restrictions

| Date | Policy | Scope |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **August 2023** | MoEnv expands plastic tableware ban to all food establishments | Cups, bowls, plates, meal boxes, inner trays — single-use plastic banned for dine-in |
| **September 2024** | Ban on single-use plastic beverage cups nationwide | All beverage sellers |
| **2024 (ongoing)** | Biodegradable plastics also banned — no industrial composting infrastructure to process them | Surprising inclusion — levels playing field for CPET reusables |
| **2025 Target** | Convenience stores must achieve 30% reusable cup adoption | Chain stores |
| **2030 Target** | 5% reduction in single-use petrochemical plastic products (2024 baseline) | All sectors |

> **Critical Insight**: Taiwan's ban on biodegradable plastic tableware is unexpectedly favorable for ReLoop. It eliminates "greenwashing" competitors (PLA cups, compostable boxes) that would compete on the narrative of sustainability without actually solving the problem.

## 🌏 Part III — Global Competitor & Analogous System Research

### 3.1 Taiwan's Existing Circular Container Players

Taiwan already has a nascent circular container ecosystem. ReLoop differentiates by targeting the **bento canteen segment specifically** — the highest-volume, most underserved sector:

| Player | Model | Key Strength | Gap ReLoop Addresses |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **好盒器 GoodToGo** | QR code borrow/return system; started in Tainan; street-food focused | Wide station network; "YouBike of containers" positioning | Primarily cups + bubble tea; weak bento/canteen coverage |
| **循拾 LooPick** | Enterprise ESG delivery + office lunch focus | Data analytics; ESG reporting | Premium corporate tier; not accessible to small bento shops |
| **青瓢 ChingPiao** | Event tableware rental (schools, corporate events) | Proven logistics for events | Not designed for daily recurring canteen use |
| **環海淨塑** | Custom ESG circular container solutions | High-touch enterprise relationships | Too bespoke; not scalable to 10,000+ bento stores |

**ReLoop's Positioning**: The missing middle — a **standardized, tech-enabled, bento-canteen-optimized** reusable system that is affordable for small stores, scalable to city-wide density, and generates ESG data as a byproduct.

### 3.2 Global Analogues

| Company | Market | Innovation | Lesson for ReLoop |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **OZZI** (USA) | University cafeterias | Token-based return kiosk; proven 90%+ return rates in campus settings | School/university channel first strategy |
| **DeliverZero** (USA) | NYC restaurant delivery | Reusable containers for food delivery; partnered with Grubhub | Delivery channel integration — partner with Foodpanda Taiwan |
| **Recup** (Germany) | Coffee cups | Cup-deposit system; 20,000+ partner locations | Network density as primary competitive moat |
| **CupClub** (UK) | Office & event cups | RFID-tracked cups; dashboard analytics | RFID + IoT tracking as premium differentiator |
| **循環生活 Circular Living** (China) | Meituan/Eleme delivery | Reusable containers in food delivery ecosystem | Integration with Taiwan delivery platforms |

**Key Pattern**: Systems that reach **>85% return rates** share three traits: (1) geographically dense return networks, (2) frictionless return process (no washing needed by user), and (3) small financial consequence for non-return (deposit, not punishment).

## 🔬 Part IV — Materials Science & LCA Research

### 4.1 Material Comparison for Reusable Bento Containers

| Material | Wash Cycles | Heat Resistance | Microwave Safe | Weight | Cost/Unit |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **Polypropylene (PP)** | ~100–200 | 100°C | Yes | Light | NT$15–30 |
| **CPET (Crystallized PET)** | **500+** | **220°C** | **Yes** | Light-medium | NT$40–70 |
| **Stainless Steel (316)** | 1,000+ | Any | No | Heavy | NT$120–200 |
| **Melamine** | 200–500 | 110°C | No | Medium-heavy | NT$25–60 |
| **Borosilicate Glass** | 500+ | 120°C | Yes | Heavy | NT$60–100 |

**ReLoop's Choice: CPET** — optimal balance of:
- High wash-cycle durability (500+) compensates for higher upfront cost
- Microwave-safe is critical for bento takeout (lunch reheating)
- 220°C heat resistance means it can survive industrial dishwashers at 85°C without degradation
- RFID embedding does not affect food safety at CPET thickness standards

### 4.2 Life Cycle Assessment (LCA) Break-Even Research

LCA break-even is the number of reuses required before a reusable container has lower total carbon footprint than the cumulative carbon of equivalent single-use containers.

| Scenario | Break-Even Uses | Post Break-Even Savings per Use |
|:---|:---|:---|
| CPET reusable vs. single-use PP tray | **~13 uses** | ~65g CO₂ avoided |
| CPET reusable vs. PE-lined paper bento box | **~8 uses** | ~80g CO₂ avoided |
| Stainless steel vs. single-use PP | ~50 uses | ~110g CO₂ avoided |
| General reusable PP vs. EPS foam | ~4–7 uses | ~55g CO₂ avoided |

> **Source**: Verive.eu LCA studies (2024); Usefull.us reusables research (2024); Zero Waste Europe comparative analysis (2024)

**Fleet Impact Modeling**:
- 1 ReLoop CPET container used 300 times/year avoids: 300 × 65g = **19.5 kg CO₂/year**
- Fleet of 1 million containers: **19,500 tonnes CO₂/year avoided**
- Equivalent to removing ~4,200 cars from Taiwan's roads annually

### 4.3 Critical System Parameters for High Return Rates

Research across global reusable container systems identifies four primary drivers of return rate performance:

| Factor | Impact on Return Rate | ReLoop Design Response |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Return station density** | High — primary driver | 500m radius max from any partner store |
| **Return friction** | High — digital frictionless beats physical kiosk | LINE Bot scan-and-return (no app download) |
| **Financial consequence** | Medium — deposit better than penalty framing | NT$150 hold (not "fine" — "forgotten deposit") |
| **Time window** | Medium — 3 days optimal | 3-day return window standard |

**Target**: 90%+ return rate within 12 months of pilot. Systems achieving <80% return rates are financially unviable; >90% are sustainably profitable.

## 💼 Part V — Business Model Deep Dive

### 5.1 Revenue Architecture

**B2B Restaurant Subscription (Primary)**

| Store Type | Monthly Fee | Value Delivered |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Small bento store (<100 meals/day) | NT$1,500/month | 200 containers, 2× weekly wash cycle |
| Mid-size bento store (100–300/day) | NT$2,500/month | 500 containers, 3× weekly wash cycle |
| Large/chain bento store (300+/day) | NT$3,000–5,000/month | 1,000+ containers, daily wash cycle + ESG report |

**B2B Corporate ESG Package (High-margin)**
- Target: 100+ employee companies ordering office lunch via delivery
- Contract: NT$8,000–20,000/month per corporate client
- Includes: Dedicated container pool, real-time ESG dashboard, quarterly carbon reduction certificate

**B2G Government/Education (Scale driver)**
- Target: Ministry of Education school cafeteria network (4,000+ schools)
- Model: Per-school subsidized deployment under circular economy mandate
- Revenue: NT$3,000–8,000/school/year for container management service

### 5.2 Cost Structure

| Cost Component | Estimate | Notes |
|:---|:---|:---|
| CPET container (amortized over 500 uses) | NT$0.08–0.14/use | NT$40–70 container cost ÷ 500 uses |
| Industrial washing (water + energy + labor) | NT$0.30–0.50/container | Per wash cycle at hub |
| Reverse logistics (collection van) | NT$0.20–0.40/container | Depends on route density |
| IoT station maintenance | NT$0.05–0.10/container | Amortized over station lifespan |
| **Total OPEX per container use** | **NT$0.63–1.14** | vs. NT$3–5 for new PP tray |

> At NT$2.00 charged per meal to restaurant (subscription amortized per container), **net margin per container use: ~NT$0.86–1.37** — a 43–68% gross margin.

### 5.3 Container Economics vs. Single-Use for Restaurants

A mid-size bento store serving 200 meals/day currently spends:
- 200 × NT$4 (PP tray + lid) = NT$800/day = **NT$24,000/month** on disposable containers

ReLoop subscription for same store:
- NT$2,500/month (subscription fee includes washing and logistics)
- **Savings: NT$21,500/month** — a compelling value proposition that sells itself.
